13 Anomalies & Flags
🔴 Null UA — 938,206 requests (0.17%)
UA string is literally (null). These are requests made by apps or clients that explicitly set no User-Agent. Cannot be attributed to any platform. Investigate at IP/session level.
🔴 "YourAppName" placeholder — 6.4M requests
An app shipped to production with the default ExoPlayer template name. This is a real app with millions of users but no proper identity in your logs. Request the developer to update the UA string.
⚠ Android 16/17 devices (2M+ requests)
Android 16 is in developer preview (released April 2025). Android 17 doesn't exist yet. These UA strings may be from modified/rooted devices with spoofed OS version numbers, or from custom AOSP builds. Treat with suspicion.
⚠ Android 5 (Lollipop) — 18.4M requests
Android 5.1.1 last received security patches in 2018. Devices on this OS are 6+ years behind on security. Your content is actively being consumed on highly vulnerable hardware.
ℹ AirPlay/2.0 (116 requests)
AirPlay metadata requests from Apple devices. Very low volume — indicates some users are casting content via AirPlay rather than native app.
ℹ Kepler/1.1 (AQVV01P) — 6 requests
Unknown device with an unfamiliar UA. "Kepler" is a codename used by various STB manufacturers. Minimal traffic — monitor for growth.

Key Takeaways
🎯 This is a CTV-first audience. Over 90% of requests come from Android-based streaming devices (Fire TV, BRAVIA, MiBox, generic Android TV). This is not a web or mobile audience.
📺 Amazon Fire TV dominates at 45%. AFTSSS and AFTSS (4K Stick models) are the single largest device cohort. Any streaming issues on these should be prioritised.
📺 Sony BRAVIA is a significant 18%. Multiple model generations active — 2015 legacy models still present alongside 2024 VU-series.
🌍 Apple TV users are English-speaking, primarily US + Australia. The locale breakdown reveals your geographic Apple TV user base clearly.
⚠️ hls2disk + FFmpeg + curl = potential stream ripping. ~1.97M combined requests from tools that can download HLS streams. This is worth investigating especially alongside the null-UA traffic.
🛡️ AdFlag is auditing your ad delivery. At 2.87% of traffic it's a significant presence — confirm this is an expected integration with your ad partners.
🔴 Palo Alto Cortex Xpanse is scanning your infrastructure. This is active reconnaissance. Your CDN endpoints are being catalogued by an external security service — could be a client's authorized scan or unwanted exposure mapping.
🔧 "YourAppName" needs to be identified and fixed. 6.4M requests from a nameless app makes attribution and debugging much harder. Contact the app's developer to update the UA.
