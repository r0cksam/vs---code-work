Channel Average Minutes: Graph Style Options

Preview only. These options use real Watch Hours audience data and are meant for choosing the final style for the Channels tab.

Metric shown: average minutes per IP for the top watch-hour channels. Style 5 also compares IP, session, and device averages together.

Style 1: Horizontal Rank Bars best for long channel names

Clean ranking view, easiest to read when many channels are visible.

Style 2: Vertical Columns compact but label-heavy

Feels like a classic chart, but long channel names can become harder to scan.

Style 3: Lollipop Rank lighter visual weight

Good when bars feel too heavy and you want a cleaner analytical look.

Style 4: Leaderboard With Mini Bars stakeholder friendly

Reads like a table and still shows relative scale. Strong option for business review.

Style 5: Grouped Comparison IP vs session vs device

Best if you want all three average-minute metrics in one place without opening three separate charts.

Alternative: Metric Cards for top performers only

How To Read This Faster abnormality guide

These checks help separate useful engagement from suspicious or unusual patterns.

High average, high hoursImportant channel behavior. This can materially affect stakeholder numbers, so validate mapping and delivery quality first.
High average, low hoursSmall audience but unusually long consumption. Good candidate for bot, test, loop, or niche audience review.
Session/device far above IPOne IP may represent many sessions/devices, or repeat playback may be concentrated. Drill into cliIP, device ID, and session ID.
Very low averageMay show quick exits, segment fetch noise, mapping mistakes, or poor playback quality.

Style 6: Deviation From Median fastest outlier view

Channels far to the red side are above normal Avg Min / IP. This catches abnormal engagement quickly.

Style 7: Heatmap compare all three averages

Dark cells mean high average minutes. A row with all dark cells deserves deeper validation.

Style 8: Scatter Impact Map volume vs abnormality

Top-right points are high average and high watch-hour impact. Red points are above the upper normal band.

Style 9: Control Band Bars normal, watch, high

This gives a simple risk label for each channel instead of only raw chart shape.

Style 10: Outlier Watchlist what to investigate first

This is the most operational style: it says which channel looks unusual, why, and what field to drill into.
